## Thesis in one sentence

**Long the operators that already chose the low-emission route; short the BF/BOF integrated mills.** CBAM (2026 binding) and TR-ETS (2026 pilot) will reprice the gap between operators' carbon intensity and their revenue. The market is already pricing this on the upside; the downside is undervalued.

## The long picks (BIST-listed, low-emission)

1. **GUBRF (Gübretaş)** — blender-only fertilizer, 3,614 TL/kg, exempt from TR-ETS pilot, near-zero CBAM exposure. Reads as "industrial chemistry" but carbon profile is more like a logistics company.

2. **BAGFS (BAGFAŞ)** — first TR fertilizer producer with operational N₂O catalyst on nitric acid; 95% process N₂O abatement. Below TR-ETS threshold. 172 TL/kg revenue per CO₂. Small market cap, big asymmetric upside if CBAM regulation rewards N₂O-controlled differently from default integrated nitric acid.

3. **TKFEN (Tekfen Holding)** — Toros Tarım subsidiary; integrated NPK + nitric acid but at ~525 kg/t vs EU default 800 kg/t. Modest upside; more an "isn't being penalized as much as the market thinks" thesis.

## The short picks (BIST-listed, high-emission)

1. **EREGL (Erdemir)** — Group Scope 1 17.3 Mt/yr at 12 TL/kg revenue per CO₂. Worst-case TR CBAM exposure: ~€200M/yr at €85/tCO₂ for EU steel exports. EU default emission factor 1.9 t/t happens to be close to actual (2.0), so iz's formula gives the operator basically no relief. Direct CBAM hit lands in 2026.

2. **KRDMD (Kardemir)** — same BF/BOF route, 10 TL/kg. Smaller operator but proportionally exposed. Also has negative TR-ETS exposure (above threshold, no clean tech).

3. **ISDMR (İsdemir)** — consolidated into EREGL; no separate trade.

## The cement segment (mixed)

Cement is in-between. EU default 1.584 t/t overshoots TR actual 0.643 by 2.5×, so all integrated TR cement makers benefit from cf-corrected MRV. But cement is downstream-protective (concrete sold domestically); CBAM hits only the export fraction.

- **AKCNS (Akçansa)** — Heidelberg + Sabancı JV; biggest EU exporter. Most CBAM benefit per TL of revenue at risk.
- **CIMSA (Çimsa)** — XSD25 member; strong ESG signal but exposure similar to Akçansa.
- **OYAKC (OYAK Çimento)** — group EF 0.685 t/t is below TR sector mean; biggest single-operator TR-ETS pilot allocation in cement.
- **NUHCM, GOLTS, AFYON, BUCIM, BSOKE** — smaller; less CBAM-export exposure.

## Caveats

- This is a **carbon-asymmetry trade**, not a directional bet on TR equities. Pair against BIST index or sector ETFs.
- TR-ETS pilot uses **100% free allocation in 2026-2027**, so direct ETS cost hits later (likely 2028+). The 2026-2027 window is for **CBAM** (export-facing), not domestic compliance.
- BAGFAŞ, Gübretaş are micro-cap (combined market cap ~€100M); position-sizing is the limiting factor.
- Erdemir is index-heavy in BIST 100 — shorting against the index has tracking error vs pure-pair trade.
- Tracenable, Climate TRACE, our bench all give different point estimates per operator; treat ±30% as our honest model uncertainty.
