# Industry association outreach

For TÜRKÇİMENTO (Turkish Cement Manufacturers' Association), Türkiye Çelik Üreticileri Derneği
(TÇÜD), TALSAD (Turkish Aluminum Industrialists Association), TGD (Turkish Fertilizer Producers Association),
and TÜSİAD's CBAM working group.

---

## Email template (English, ~200 words)

**Subject:** Open per-facility emissions benchmark for TR CBAM industry — request for endorsement / methodology review

> Hello [Association],
>
> I'm writing to introduce a public, Apache-2.0-licensed per-facility emissions benchmark for
> Turkish CBAM-scope industry, with the aim of seeking your association's review and (if
> appropriate) endorsement.
>
> **TR-MRV-Bench v0** ([iz-b0n.pages.dev](https://iz-b0n.pages.dev)) is a one-person
> contribution: 59 Turkish CBAM-scope facilities with 21 audit-grade Scope 1 numbers cited to
> operator-published TSRS / IAR / ISO 14064-1 reports. A closed-form formula
> (`capacity × emission-factor × capacity-factor`) reproduces audited Scope 1 within ±20% on
> 21 plants — **+85.3% log-MAE reduction vs the EU CBAM default**.
>
> **Why your association.** If every TR CBAM-scope operator used real per-facility data
> instead of paying the EU default, our estimate is ~€2 billion per year in CBAM payments
> stays in Turkey instead of going to the EU treasury. This number can only be captured if
> mid-tier operators have access to a defensible per-facility baseline — which the bench is
> designed to provide. Your endorsement would dramatically increase its uptake.
>
> Three asks:
> 1. **Methodology review.** All EF / cf priority rules are in the repo; we'd value
>    domain-expert critique on any sector defaults that mismatch TR reality.
> 2. **Per-plant disclosure encouragement.** Several TR operators in your sector publish
>    only group totals. Your association is well-positioned to nudge per-plant disclosure
>    (we already see this in the TSRS rollout — it could be extended to your members).
> 3. **Citation in association communications / CBAM working group materials.**
>
> No commercial ask. Apache-2.0, no NDA, no fees. The arXiv-style preprint draft is at
> [paper/iz_v0.md](https://github.com/abgnydn/iz/blob/master/paper/iz_v0.md).
>
> Happy to present in person or virtually at a working group meeting.
>
> Best regards,
> Ahmet Barış Günaydın
> hi@barisgunaydin.com · barisgunaydin.com

## Per-association notes

- **TÜRKÇİMENTO**: their 2023 sector report gave us the 0.643 t/t cement EF that anchors our
  cement baseline. They have direct relationships with all major TR cement operators. Cement
  is also our largest sector by plant count (34 facilities, 7 audit-grade). Highest-leverage
  association to pursue first.

- **TÇÜD (Türkiye Çelik Üreticileri Derneği)**: covers Erdemir, İsdemir, Kardemir, Habaş,
  Çolakoğlu, İzdemir, İçdaş, Tosyalı, Diler, Yazıcı, Ekinciler, MMK Atakaş, Kroman, Borçelik.
  Steel is our most-disclosed sector but also has the structural BF/BOF n=3 problem. They
  could help unlock per-plant disclosures from Tosyalı Holding and the private mills.

- **TALSAD**: 3 aluminum facilities in our bench (Eti primary, Assan + ASAŞ downstream).
  Eti Alüminyum is the biggest gap — only primary smelter in TR, no public Scope 1.

- **TGD**: covers Toros, BAGFAŞ, Gübretaş, Gemlik Gübre. Mid-size sector for our bench but
  the EU default overstates by 20-90× depending on plant type — highest theoretical impact
  per facility.

- **TÜSİAD CBAM working group**: cross-sector. If they pick up the bench as a reference,
  uptake across industries goes up at once.

---

## Tone

- Frame as offer of contribution, not request for endorsement-as-blessing.
- "Methodology review" is the entry — let them critique the EFs and route maps first.
- Be ready to incorporate corrections. Their domain experts may catch things our PDFs missed.
- The €2B/yr number is in the email; don't double down on it in the first meeting.
  Let them ask.

---

## What success looks like

- **Win**: a TÜRKÇİMENTO or TÜSİAD logo on a CBAM working group document that cites iz.
- **Realistic**: a single methodology review email exchange where they suggest 2-3
  parameter changes; iz incorporates them; the bench is cited in a single industry newsletter.
- **Loss**: silence. Move on to next association after 3 weeks.
