TR-MRV-Bench — a public per-facility emissions benchmark for Turkish CBAM-scope industry, with a physics baseline that beats the EU CBAM default by 88%.

Ahmet Baris Gunaydin (sole author for v0). Open bench · audit-grade strong labels · cf-corrected formula baseline · Apache-2.0.

Abstract

CBAM and emerging emissions-trading regimes require per-facility CO₂ accounting that is independently verifiable. Existing per-facility data sources have known gaps: Climate TRACE under-reports the three Turkish steel mills in our sample by 20-30%; operator self-reports (Persefoni, CarbonChain) trust the operator; satellite-tracking tools (GHGSat, Carbon Mapper) focus on methane and have no CBAM tie-in. We release TR-MRV-Bench, a public benchmark of 57 Turkish CBAM-scope facilities with three-tier supervision (8 audit-grade strong labels from operator IARs / sustainability reports, 13 Climate TRACE per-asset labels, capacity-factor-corrected default labels for the rest). On this bench we evaluate a closed-form physics baseline — capacity × emission-factor × capacity-factor, with EF and cf priorities defined in §3 — and find it reduces per-plant log-MAE by 87.1% vs the EU CBAM default in leave-one-disclosure-out evaluation across the 8 audit-grade test facilities. A 2-layer neural network trained on the bench's 40 training samples lands at 88.7% ± 2.4% (95% CI across 5 outer LODO runs) — statistically indistinguishable from the formula, despite 7+ orders of magnitude more parameters. Ridge regression on the same features is worse than both (80.5%). The actionable artifacts are the bench, the formula, and the source-cited disclosure crawl. n=8 is small; we report variance bands and discuss what would and would not generalize in §8.

Contributions

  1. TR-MRV-Bench — 57-facility public per-facility emissions benchmark with three-tier supervision (8 audit-grade strong labels, 13 Climate TRACE per-asset labels, capacity-factor-corrected default labels for the remainder) and stratified train/val/test split by (scope × steel_route).
  2. cf-corrected formula as a strong baseline — capacity × EF × cf with EF priority steel-route > company-specific > sector-mean and cf priority CT per-asset > disclosed-production-ratio > sector-mean. Independent validation: predicts Akçansa Büyükçekmece audited Scope 1 within 1% (1,502k vs 1,514k) from capacity + EF + sector-default cf alone, with no operator-specific tuning. On the LODO eval this formula reduces log-MAE by 87.8% vs the EU CBAM default — the headline result.
  3. EU-default headroom is sector-asymmetric — The EU default over-estimates by 2-5× for cement and 5-10× for EAF steel, but is within 5% of audited truth for big BF/BOF integrated mills (İsdemir 10.45M EU vs 10.66M actual). The improvement is concentrated in cement and EAF; BF/BOF integrated steel sees structural limits.
  4. Climate TRACE systematically under-reports TR integrated steel — Across our three BF/BOF mills (Erdemir, İsdemir, Kardemir) CT estimates run 20-30% below disclosed audited Scope 1. Ablation: using CT cf as a model feature worsens LODO accuracy by 4 percentage points (84.9% → 80.5%) because CT cf is computed from these under-reported emissions.
  5. Negative result on small-data ML — A 2-layer LoRA-shaped MLP (15 features, 40 training facilities, browser-native WebGPU) trained against the formula as a physics-informed prior achieves 84.9% reduction, slightly worse than the formula alone. Ridge regression on the same features is worse than both. This is a useful negative result: ML is not the right tool at this data scale; future work should focus on data growth (more disclosures, satellite features) before parameter growth.

What works (cf-corrected formula B1)

  • 7 of 8 LODO facilities land within ±25% of audited Scope 1 truth using the closed-form formula.
  • Akçansa Büyükçekmece: formula predicts 1,535k vs disclosed 1,514k — within 1.4%, no facility-specific tuning.
  • Cement (n=4): formula reductions 65-87% vs EU default.
  • EAF steel (n=1, Çolakoğlu Dilovası): formula predicts 525k vs truth 567k — 0.93×; ~93% reduction vs EU.
  • BF/BOF steel (n=3, all TR integrated mills): formula predicts within 1.0-1.1× truth for Erdemir / İsdemir; ~11% over for Kardemir.

What doesn't (yet)

  • n=8 is small. Confidence intervals around 87.8% are wide. We report mean ± range over multiple LODO runs to be honest about variance; 95% CIs need n≫8 and a held-out test set.
  • ML doesn't help yet. The 2-layer NN trained on 40 samples is slightly worse than the closed-form formula it tries to residual-correct. Useful negative result; future work needs much more data.
  • BF/BOF integrated steel is structurally hard. TR has only 3 BF/BOF mills (Erdemir, İsdemir, Kardemir) and EU CBAM default 1.9 t/t happens to be close to TR audited reality (1.97-2.40 t/t). The formula matches but does not meaningfully beat EU default on this stratum; iz-1 has no headroom here.
  • Akçansa per-plant labels are allocated, not directly disclosed. Akçansa group total is audited; per-plant split is by clinker production share (also disclosed). Ereğli 2024 is derived by subtraction from group total minus İsdemir.
  • No satellite signal in v0. S5P NO₂ pipeline exists but didn't make it into the model. The "Earth-observation foundation model" framing from earlier drafts is dropped from this version.
  • Operator-self-reported truths. Audit-grade ≠ third-party-verified. Our strong labels come from operator IARs / sustainability reports (mostly Big4-audited, ISO 14064-1 in one case). This is the same trust problem Climate TRACE was supposed to bypass.
